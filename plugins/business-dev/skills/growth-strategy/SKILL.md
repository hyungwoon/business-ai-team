# Growth Strategy Skill

Frameworks for business development, partnership strategy, and new market entry for Korean and global business contexts.

## Business Opportunity Assessment Framework

### 1. Market Sizing (TAM / SAM / SOM)

| Level | Definition | Method |
|---|---|---|
| TAM (Total Addressable Market) | Total revenue opportunity if 100% market share | Top-down: industry reports, macro data |
| SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market) | Portion you can realistically reach | Bottom-up: segment × price × volume |
| SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market) | Realistic capture in 1-3 years | Benchmarks from comparable entrants |

**Calculation template:**
```
TAM = Total market units × Average price
SAM = TAM × addressable segment %
SOM = SAM × realistic market share % (year 1-3)
```

### 2. Opportunity Scoring Matrix

Score each opportunity on five dimensions (1-5 scale):

| Dimension | Weight | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Market size | 25% | TAM/SAM scale relative to current business |
| Strategic fit | 25% | Alignment with core capabilities and brand |
| Competitive advantage | 20% | Defensibility — moats, IP, relationships |
| Time to revenue | 15% | Speed from deal to first dollar |
| Risk level | 15% | Regulatory, execution, partner dependency |

**Score:** Sum of (dimension score × weight). Prioritize opportunities scoring ≥ 3.5.

## Partnership Framework

### Partnership Types

| Type | Structure | Best For |
|---|---|---|
| Reseller | Partner sells your product for margin | Fast distribution, low upfront cost |
| Co-sell | Joint sales motion with revenue share | Enterprise deals requiring warm intros |
| Technology integration | API/product integration | Ecosystem stickiness, developer reach |
| OEM / White-label | Your tech under partner's brand | Market access with limited brand build |
| Strategic alliance | Joint GTM + co-investment | Major market entry, shared risk |

### Partnership Evaluation Criteria

Before entering any partnership, assess:

1. **Reach** — Does the partner access customers you cannot reach alone?
2. **Credibility** — Does their brand elevate yours?
3. **Alignment** — Are incentives truly aligned, or will conflict emerge?
4. **Exclusivity risk** — Does exclusivity close off better options?
5. **Dependency risk** — If this partner leaves, what is the fallback?

### Partnership Term Sheet Essentials

- Revenue share / commission rate and payment cadence
- Exclusivity scope (geography, segment, duration)
- Minimum performance commitments (MPC) with cure periods
- Intellectual property ownership and licensing terms
- Termination rights and notice periods
- Co-marketing budget and approval rights
- Data sharing and confidentiality obligations

## Go-To-Market (GTM) Planning

### GTM Motion Selection

| Motion | Best When | Key Metric |
|---|---|---|
| Product-led growth (PLG) | Low ACV, high volume, self-serve product | Free → paid conversion rate |
| Sales-led growth (SLG) | High ACV, complex deals, long cycle | Pipeline coverage, win rate |
| Partner-led growth | Distribution gaps, trust barriers | Partner-sourced revenue % |
| Community-led growth | Strong user community, advocacy potential | Community NPS, referral rate |

### GTM Launch Checklist

**Pre-launch (T-90 to T-30):**
- [ ] ICP (Ideal Customer Profile) defined with 3-5 firmographic + behavioral attributes
- [ ] Positioning and messaging validated with 10+ target customer interviews
- [ ] Pricing model finalized (value-based, cost-plus, or competitive)
- [ ] Sales playbook drafted with objection handling
- [ ] Channel partners identified and onboarded

**Launch (T-30 to T-0):**
- [ ] Sales enablement training complete
- [ ] CRM pipeline stages configured
- [ ] Marketing campaigns scheduled (PR, content, paid)
- [ ] Success metrics and dashboards live

**Post-launch (T+30 to T+90):**
- [ ] Win/loss analysis from first 20 deals
- [ ] ICP refinement based on early customer data
- [ ] Pricing model adjustments if needed
- [ ] Partner performance review

## Investor & Executive Communication

### Business Case Structure

A compelling business case for new initiatives follows this structure:

1. **Situation** — What is the current state and why does it matter now?
2. **Opportunity** — Market size, timing, why we are positioned to win
3. **Proposal** — Specific initiative with scope and success criteria
4. **Investment** — Resources required (people, budget, time)
5. **Return** — Financial projections with assumptions clearly stated
6. **Risks** — Top 3 risks and mitigation plans
7. **Ask** — Clear decision requested with timeline

### Financial Projection Template

```

Revenue:   ₩___M    ₩___M    ₩___M

Key assumptions:
- CAC: ₩___
- LTV: ₩___
- Churn: ___%/year
- Payback period: ___ months
```

## Korean Market Specifics

### Business Culture Considerations

- **관계 (Gwanye) first:** Relationships precede transactions. Budget time for informal meetings (식사, 회식) before business discussions.
- **의사결정 구조:** Many Korean enterprises require senior executive (임원) sign-off. Map decision-makers early.
- **RFP 관행:** Large enterprises often use formal RFP processes. Prepare detailed proposal documents (제안서) with clear pricing tables.
- **현지화 요구사항:** Technology partners often expected to have Korean-language support, local data residency, and Korean engineering contacts.

### Common Partnership Entry Points in Korea

| Sector | Entry Strategy |
|---|---|
| 대기업 (Chaebol) | System integrator (SI) partnership or CVC/accelerator program |
| 중소기업 | Direct sales or regional distributor |
| 스타트업 | Co-founder intros, accelerator networks (TIPS, Born2Global) |
| 정부/공공기관 | 조달청 (KONEPS) registration, G2B procurement process |

## Deal Flow Management Pipeline

### Stage Definitions

| Stage | Definition | Exit Criteria |
|---|---|---|
| **Lead** | Identified prospect, no contact made | Contact info verified, ICP match confirmed |
| **Qualify** | Initial conversation held | Budget, authority, need, timeline (BANT) assessed |
| **Proposal** | Formal proposal submitted | Proposal received and acknowledged by decision-maker |
| **Negotiate** | Terms under active discussion | Redlines exchanged, legal review initiated |
| **Close** | Contract signed, deal live | Signed agreement + kickoff date confirmed |

### Pipeline Health Metrics

Track these weekly to catch stalls before they become losses:

```
Pipeline coverage ratio = Total pipeline value / Quarterly target
Target: 3-4x coverage

Stage conversion rates (benchmark):
  Lead → Qualify:    40-60%
  Qualify → Proposal: 50-70%
  Proposal → Negotiate: 60-80%
  Negotiate → Close:  70-90%

Average deal cycle by type:
  SMB partnership:    30-60 days
  Mid-market:         60-120 days
  Enterprise / 대기업: 180-365 days
```

### Deal Review Template (Weekly)

## Due Diligence Checklist

Use for partnerships, M&A targets, and major vendor relationships.

### Commercial Due Diligence
- [ ] Revenue breakdown by product, customer, geography (3-year trend)
- [ ] Top 10 customer concentration — any single customer > 20% of revenue?
- [ ] Customer churn rate and NPS / CSAT scores
- [ ] Pipeline quality and sales cycle data
- [ ] Pricing model and gross margin by segment
- [ ] Competitive positioning and win/loss data

### Legal & Regulatory Due Diligence
- [ ] Corporate structure and ownership (지분 구조, 주주명부)
- [ ] Pending litigation or regulatory investigations
- [ ] IP ownership — patents, trademarks, software licenses
- [ ] Key contracts — change-of-control clauses, exclusivity, non-competes
- [ ] Employment agreements for key personnel
- [ ] Korean regulatory licenses (사업자등록증, sector-specific permits)

### Financial Due Diligence
- [ ] Audited financial statements (3 years)
- [ ] Cash flow and burn rate (for startups)
- [ ] Deferred revenue and backlog
- [ ] Off-balance-sheet liabilities
- [ ] Tax compliance — 법인세, 부가가치세 filings current

### Technical Due Diligence (for tech partnerships)
- [ ] Architecture review — scalability, security posture
- [ ] Code quality and technical debt assessment
- [ ] Data handling and PIPA compliance
- [ ] Uptime / SLA history
- [ ] Key engineering personnel dependency

## Revenue Attribution Model

Track where revenue originates to optimize channel investment.

| Attribution Type | Definition | Measurement |
|---|---|---|
| **Direct** | Closed by internal sales, no partner involvement | CRM opportunity source = Direct |
| **Partner-sourced** | Partner identified and introduced the opportunity | CRM opportunity source = Partner name |
| **Partner-influenced** | Partner assisted but did not originate | CRM influence field tagged |
| **Channel** | Sold through reseller / distributor | CRM opportunity type = Channel |

## Korean 대기업 Engagement Playbook

### 의사결정 구조 이해

Korean conglomerates (대기업) have layered approval structures. A deal that looks agreed at the working level can stall for months without executive alignment.

| Level | Title | Role in Decision |
|---|---|---|
| 실무자 | 대리 / 과장 | Evaluates technical fit, prepares internal report |
| 중간관리자 | 차장 / 부장 | Champions internally, owns budget justification |
| 임원 | 이사 / 상무 / 전무 | Final approval authority for most deals |
| C-레벨 | 부사장 / 사장 / 부회장 | Required for strategic partnerships, M&A |

**Rule:** Always map all three levels. Winning the 실무자 without the 임원 means the deal dies in committee.

### 제안서 구성 (Proposal Structure for Korean Enterprises)

Korean enterprise proposals follow a formal structure. Deviating from this format signals inexperience.

```
표지 (Cover)
  - 제안서 제목
  - 제안사 회사명 + 로고
  - 제안일자
  - 수신: [대기업명] [부서명] 귀중

목차 (Table of Contents)

1. 제안 배경 및 목적
   - 현황 분석 (고객사 현재 상황)
   - 제안 배경 (왜 지금인가)

2. 제안 솔루션 개요
   - 핵심 기능 및 차별점
   - 기술 아키텍처 (다이어그램 포함)

3. 기대 효과
   - 정량적 효과 (비용 절감, 매출 증대 — 수치 필수)
   - 정성적 효과

4. 추진 일정 (Gantt chart)

5. 사업 조건
   - 계약 구조
   - 가격 테이블 (VAT 별도 명시)
   - 지불 조건

6. 회사 소개 및 레퍼런스
   - 주요 고객사 및 사례
   - 인증 및 수상 이력

7. 별첨 (Appendix)
```

### SI (System Integrator) 경로 전략

Most large Korean enterprises procure technology through SI partners (삼성SDS, LG CNS, SK C&C, 롯데정보통신, etc.) rather than directly from vendors.

**SI 파트너십 진입 단계:**

1. **SI 파트너 등록** — 각 SI사의 파트너 포털에 솔루션 등록 (기술 검증 필요)
2. **공동 레퍼런스 구축** — SI와 함께 파일럿 프로젝트 수행, 공동 케이스 스터디 작성
3. **제안 협력** — SI가 대기업 RFP에 응찰 시 솔루션 포함 요청 (제안서 공동 작성)
4. **수익 배분 협의** — 일반적으로 SI 마진 20-35%, 솔루션 벤더 65-80%

**주요 SI별 파트너 프로그램:**

| SI | 파트너 프로그램 | 특이사항 |
|---|---|---|
| 삼성SDS | SDS 파트너 에코시스템 | 삼성 계열사 우선 공급 |
| LG CNS | CNS 파트너 포털 | LG 그룹사 + 공공 강점 |
| SK C&C | SK 테크엑스 파트너 | SK 계열사 + 금융 강점 |
| 롯데정보통신 | 롯데 IT 파트너 | 유통/리테일 특화 |

## Strategic Alliance Governance Framework

### Alliance Lifecycle Stages

```
1. Explore (0-3 months)
   - NDA signed
   - Joint opportunity assessment
   - Executive alignment meeting

2. Formalize (1-2 months)
   - Term sheet / MOU signed
   - Legal review of full agreement
   - Contract execution

3. Launch (1-3 months)
   - Joint GTM plan finalized
   - Sales enablement completed
   - First co-sell opportunity identified

4. Scale (ongoing)
   - Quarterly business reviews (QBR)
   - Joint pipeline reviews (monthly)
   - Co-marketing campaigns

5. Renew or Exit
   - Annual performance review vs. MPC
   - Renegotiate terms or trigger exit clause
```

### Quarterly Business Review (QBR) Agenda

```
Alliance QBR — [PARTNER NAME] — [QUARTER]

1. Performance vs. commitments (15 min)
   - Revenue: actual vs. target
   - Pipeline: coverage and quality
   - Joint wins and losses

2. Market feedback (15 min)
   - Customer feedback on joint solution
   - Competitive intelligence

3. Enablement and support (10 min)
   - Training completion
   - Support ticket trends

4. Next quarter plan (15 min)
   - Joint campaigns
   - Target accounts
   - Product roadmap alignment

5. Issues and escalations (5 min)
```

## Reference Selling & Case Study Methodology

### Reference Customer Program

Reference customers are your most powerful sales asset. Manage them deliberately.

**Reference tiers:**

| Tier | Commitment | Benefit to Customer |
|---|---|---|
| Bronze | Written case study approval | Early access to features |
| Silver | Written case study + analyst call | Dedicated CSM, priority support |
| Gold | Written case study + sales calls + speaking | Executive relationship, co-marketing budget |

### Case Study Template

```
CUSTOMER: [COMPANY NAME]
INDUSTRY: [SECTOR]
SIZE: [EMPLOYEES / REVENUE]

THE CHALLENGE
[2-3 sentences: what problem did they have before?
Be specific — include metrics if possible.]

THE SOLUTION
[2-3 sentences: what did they implement and how?
Focus on the specific features/approach used.]

THE RESULTS
- [Metric 1]: [Before] → [After] ([% change])
- [Metric 2]: [Before] → [After] ([% change])
- [Qualitative outcome]: [Quote from customer]

CUSTOMER QUOTE
"[Direct quote from named executive, title, company]"

ABOUT [COMPANY NAME]
[1-2 sentence company description]
```

### Reference Call Facilitation Guide

Before the call:
- [ ] Brief the reference customer on the prospect's specific concerns
- [ ] Share the prospect's company background with the reference
- [ ] Confirm the reference is comfortable discussing specific metrics

During the call:
- [ ] Introduce both parties, then step back
- [ ] Have a list of suggested questions ready if conversation stalls
- [ ] Do not interrupt or correct the reference customer

After the call:
- [ ] Send thank-you note to reference customer within 24 hours
- [ ] Debrief with prospect on key takeaways
- [ ] Log the reference call in CRM (track usage to avoid over-tapping)
